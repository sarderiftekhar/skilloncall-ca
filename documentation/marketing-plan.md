## Complete Marketing Plan for Canada

### Phase 1: Pre-Launch Foundation (Months 1-2)
**Budget Allocation: $1,500**

#### 1.1 Brand Assets & Content Creation ($800)
- Professional photography: Local workers and employers (stock photos acceptable initially)
- Video testimonials: 3-5 short videos (30-60 seconds)
- Bilingual marketing materials: Flyers, posters, business cards
- Social media templates: Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn posts
- Email templates: Welcome sequences, newsletters

#### 1.2 Website SEO Foundation ($400)
- Keyword research: "on-demand workers Canada", "shift work Toronto", "flexible staffing"
- On-page SEO optimization: Meta descriptions, alt tags, schema markup
- Google Business Profile setup
- Local citations: Yelp, Yellow Pages, Canadian business directories

#### 1.3 Social Media Setup ($300)
- Professional profiles: Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok
- Content calendar: 3 months planned
- Bilingual social media strategy
- Community engagement plan

---

### Phase 2: Launch Campaign (Months 2-3)
**Budget Allocation: $3,500**

#### 2.1 Digital Advertising ($2,000)
**Facebook & Instagram Ads:**
- Target: Toronto GTA initially, then expand
- Employer campaigns: $1,000
  - Demographics: Business owners, managers, 25-55 years
  - Interests: Restaurants, retail, small business
  - Lookalike audiences from early signups
- Worker campaigns: $1,000
  - Demographics: 18-45 years, service industry workers
  - Interests: Part-time work, flexible jobs, gig economy
  - Retargeting website visitors

**Google Ads (Search & Display):**
- Budget: $500
- Keywords: "hire workers Toronto", "find shift work", "on-demand staffing"
- Local search ads for high-intent queries

**LinkedIn Ads (B2B for Employers):**
- Budget: $500
- Target: Small business owners, restaurant managers, retail managers
- Sponsored content promoting free trial

#### 2.2 Local Community Marketing ($1,000)
**Toronto-First Strategy:**
- Flyer distribution: $300
  - High-traffic areas: Restaurants, cafes, retail districts
  - Community centers, libraries, transit stations
  - Bilingual flyers (English/French)

- Local partnerships: $400
  - Restaurant associations
  - Small business networks
  - Chamber of Commerce memberships
  - Trade associations (food service, retail)

- Event participation: $300
  - Local business fairs
  - Job fairs (for workers)
  - Community events
  - Booth setup, promotional materials

#### 2.3 Content Marketing ($500)
- Blog content: 8-10 articles (bilingual)
  - "How to Hire Staff Quickly in Toronto"
  - "Finding Flexible Work in the Service Industry"
  - "Canadian Labor Laws for Shift Workers"
- SEO-focused content to drive organic traffic
- Guest posting on Canadian business blogs

## Conclusion

This marketing plan is designed to maximize impact within a $10,000 budget by focusing on cost-effective digital channels, local community engagement, and organic growth strategies. The plan emphasizes SkillOnCall.ca's Canadian identity, bilingual support, and service industry specialization while building both sides of the marketplace (employers and workers) simultaneously.

Key success factors:
- Consistent execution across all channels
- Regular monitoring and optimization
- Strong focus on local Toronto market initially
- Bilingual content and support
- Emphasis on trust, compliance, and Canadian values
- Building community and word-of-mouth referrals

The plan is flexible and should be adjusted based on performance data, market feedback, and changing business priorities.
